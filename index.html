How It Works

What the Service Includes

The service is delivered across your first three days in Munich. The exact order may vary depending on your arrival time, opening hours and what needs to be completed first.

Airport to Accommodation

I meet you at Munich Airport, help you manage your luggage and travel with you to your accommodation by public transport. During the journey, I explain the ticket options and how to use Munich's transport network.

SIM Card and Initial Setup

We review SIM card options, the identification you may need and the steps involved in getting connected. I also help you understand which immediate tasks need attention after you arrive.

Banking

I explain the usual routes and documents for opening a student bank account. You choose the provider, complete the application and sign any agreement yourself.

City Registration

I explain how city registration works, which documents you need and how to look for an appointment. Appointments in Munich are limited and are often booked well in advance, so registration may take place after the three days. You attend the appointment and complete the registration yourself.

Health Insurance

I explain how student health insurance usually works, which documents may be required and how to begin the application process. You choose the insurer, complete the application and sign any agreement yourself.

These areas are covered across the agreed three days, with the order adjusted to suit your arrival time and opening hours. Some appointments, such as city registration, may fall outside the three days depending on availability.

Pricing

The Pilot Fee

€30

The introductory fee is €30 for support across the agreed three days. No payment is collected through the inquiry form. I first confirm availability and the service details with you.

You pay when we meet in Munich, in cash or by PayPal. Nothing is paid through this website, and no card or bank details are ever requested.

  • Meeting at Munich Airport
  • Help managing your luggage
  • Public transport ticket explanation
  • Travel to your accommodation
  • Help understanding SIM card options
  • Information about banking and health insurance processes
  • Guidance on city registration and finding an appointment
  • Help with questions during the agreed three days
Costs charged by other providers are not included. Students pay these directly:
  • Public transport tickets
  • SIM card and mobile plan
  • Bank fees, where applicable
  • Health insurance premiums
  • Food and personal purchases
  • Taxi or other external services

B.O.B provides general information and practical help. It is not a taxi, transfer service or regulated legal, immigration, tax, financial or insurance adviser. More detail is in the FAQs.

About B.O.B

Meet Baljit

BB

When I moved to Munich as an international student, I quickly realised that the hardest part was not getting accepted to university, it was everything that came after. From German bureaucracy to finding accommodation and settling into a new city, I had to work it all out on my own.

That is why I created B.O.B. My goal is to make those first days in Munich easier and less stressful for international students.
