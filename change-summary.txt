B.O.B — CHANGE SUMMARY
=======================
Goal: make the service understandable within the first few seconds.

TWO DECISIONS YOU CONFIRMED
- Kept your recent headline "Welcome to Munich. Let's get you started." and the
  button label "Request Arrival Support", rather than reverting to the older
  wording the brief listed.
- Kept the five service cards, including City Registration. The new quick-overview
  journey deliberately covers only the five core stages and does not include city
  registration, exactly as the brief asked.

HERO COPY — UPDATED
Paragraph now reads: "I meet you at Munich Airport, accompany you to your
accommodation and help you understand the main practical tasks involved in
getting started, including public transport, your SIM card, banking and health
insurance." The headline and both buttons are unchanged; the secondary button
now scrolls to the new journey (#journey).

VISUAL SERVICE JOURNEY — ADDED
A new section sits directly below the hero and above the detailed cards.
Heading: "From Arrival to Getting Started".
Introduction: "A clear overview of how B.O.B supports you during your first days
in Munich."
FIVE STAGES, each with an inline line-SVG icon (no emoji, no icon library):
1. Meet at Munich Airport — I meet you at the agreed location when you arrive.
2. Travel to Your Accommodation — We travel together by public transport and I
   explain the ticket options.
3. SIM Card and Connectivity — We review SIM card options and the steps needed to
   get connected.
4. Banking Process — I explain the usual documents and account opening process.
5. Health Insurance Process — I explain how student health insurance works and how
   to begin the application.
The descriptions stay short; the fuller detail lives in the cards below.

DESKTOP JOURNEY BEHAVIOUR
Five equal stages in one horizontal row, icons in consistent rounded containers,
a thin grey connecting line behind the icon row and a restrained blue progress
line that draws left to right. Calm, not busy; the section is quieter than the
hero.

MOBILE JOURNEY BEHAVIOUR
Below 760px the row becomes a vertical timeline: icon on the left, text on the
right, a thin vertical line linking the icons, comfortable spacing, no horizontal
scrolling.

ANIMATION BEHAVIOUR
Plays once when the section enters the viewport. Each stage fades in with a small
upward movement, staggered by roughly 0.1s, and the connecting line reveals
progressively. After it completes every stage stays visible. No looping,
bouncing, pulsing, rotating, parallax or video.

REDUCED MOTION
Under prefers-reduced-motion all stages and the full line show immediately with
no movement. A <noscript> fallback also shows the whole journey (and other
revealed content) when JavaScript is unavailable, so the overview is always
readable.

SERVICE CARD HIERARCHY — CHANGED
Each of the five cards now has a short bold summary line between the heading and
the paragraph, in a slightly stronger weight than the body but lighter than the
heading:
- Airport to Accommodation: Meet at Munich Airport and travel together to your
  accommodation.
- SIM Card and Initial Setup: Understand your SIM card options and get connected.
- Banking: Understand the usual documents and account opening process.
- City Registration: Understand how registration works and how to find an
  appointment.
- Health Insurance: Understand student health insurance and how to begin the
  application.
No icons were added to the cards; they already use images.

CONFIRMATIONS
- No testimonials, reviews, ratings, stars, student photos, quotes, "trusted by"
  claims, student counts, university endorsements or logos were added.
- City registration was not added to the journey and is not presented as an
  official procedure handled by B.O.B.
- No external library, framework, font or video was added; only HTML, CSS and a
  small IntersectionObserver in plain JavaScript.
- No required functionality was intentionally removed. Navigation, mobile menu,
  anchors, pricing, founder, FAQ modal, inquiry form, Formspree endpoint,
  SessionStorage, referral tracking, conditional WhatsApp logic, success and
  failure states, the sticky mobile button, legal links, image paths, reduced
  motion support and the noindex setting are all preserved.
- JavaScript passes a syntax check and no test submission was sent.
