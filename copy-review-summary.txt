B.O.B ARRIVAL SUPPORT — COPY REVIEW SUMMARY
============================================

THE NEW BRAND VOICE
The site now speaks as the founder, in the first person, in calm British
English. Sentences are short and concrete: they describe what happens on each
day rather than making claims about how the service will feel. Promotional
framing, sentimental storytelling and defensive over-explanation were removed.
The voice is the same on the homepage, the booking page and in every system
message, so the site reads as one person writing, not a marketing team.

MAIN COPY PROBLEMS CORRECTED
- The phrase "personal arrival and settling-in support" was used as the
  description in the hero, meta tags, trust bar and footer. Each place now
  describes the service in its own natural words.
- The hero paragraph opened with a generic emotional claim ("Arriving in a new
  country can feel overwhelming"). It now states what actually happens.
- Repetition of "support", "personal", "essential", "guidance" and "practical"
  across neighbouring sections was thinned out.
- AI-flavoured phrases were removed: "Nothing is too small to ask", "built by
  someone who's been in your seat", "the support I wish I had", "Those first
  days shaped everything that came after", "Want to say hi right away?",
  "Thanks!".
- The founder section was cut from four storytelling paragraphs to three
  factual ones and the quotation was rewritten to a single restrained line.
- The long service-limitation paragraph in pricing was shortened and split
  into a costs list plus one compact boundary paragraph.
- FAQ answers now answer the question in the first sentence and stop sooner.
  "How did my university know about B.O.B?" became "Does my university share
  my information with B.O.B?" with a two-sentence answer.
- Success and failure messages were rewritten professionally ("Inquiry
  received" / "Your inquiry could not be sent").
- Section labels are now consistently uppercase: HOW IT WORKS, PRICING,
  ABOUT B.O.B, FAQ, PLAN YOUR ARRIVAL. No label above the hero.

SENTENCES STILL REQUIRING LEGAL REVIEW
- The pricing boundary paragraph ("B.O.B offers help and general information,
  not regulated advice…") and the matching FAQ answer "What is not included
  in the service?" — a professional should confirm these disclaimers are
  sufficient under German law.
- The FAQ answer "Can I cancel after booking?" ("Nothing is binding before
  that point.") — depends on the cancellation terms a professional still
  needs to draft.
- The whole Privacy Policy and Impressum remain templates pending
  professional review, as marked in their HTML comments. The Impressum
  retains the previously specified legal service description ("Personal
  arrival and settling-in support for international students arriving in
  Munich"), which is intentionally more formal than the marketing copy.

FUNCTIONALITY CONFIRMATION
No functionality was intentionally changed. Element IDs, form field names,
the Formspree inquiry endpoint, the booking-endpoint placeholder and its
submission guard, referral tracking, sessionStorage draft saving, conditional
WhatsApp validation and all accessibility attributes are unchanged.
JavaScript on both pages passes a syntax check, and an automated scan
confirms all form IDs and field names are intact, €30 appears only in the
pricing section and cost FAQ, and no banned phrases remain in public copy.
